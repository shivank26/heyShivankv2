Aghori in Varanasi

Varanasi, also known as Banaras or Kashi is a holy city in Uttar Pradesh, India. Surrounded by the holy river Ganga, this city is one of the major hindu devotees. Being the land of Kashi Vishvanath, this place is one of the major attraction for Aghori. The Aghori (Sanskrit aghora) are a small group of ascetic Shaiva sadhus. They engage in post-mortem rituals. They often dwell in charnel grounds, smear cremation ashes on their bodies, and use bones from human corpses for crafting kapalas (skull cups which Shiva and other Hindu deities are often iconically depicted holding or using) and jewelry. Their practices are contradictory to orthodox Hinduism.
